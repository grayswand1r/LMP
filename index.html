About

Lynsey Makay Photography is owned and operated by Lynsey Turner. Lynsey was born in Williamsport, Pennsylvania- but has spent most of her life in Atlanta, Georgia. Photography is something that has always been close to her heart and has played an active role throughout her entire life. Lately, she has been drawn to lifestyle, lowlight, and nature photography. It is her goal to provide high-quality images capable of capturing life’s most cherished moments through the art of photography. She works to continually improve the quality of her work by investing in continued education, maintaining a fluent understanding of lighting techniques and diving into other elements of media such as videography.

What a Wonderful World

There is something special about walking around campus especially as of late. The semester is ending. Education holds a special place in my heart in many ways. Not just when I am sitting in class, but recently I have been making moves to stay educated in ways that are so valuable for my total life goals. I have been taking time to educate my self on some really incredible open source (free) software. Can I just start by saying I LOVE GIMP. If you�re unfamiliar-which after struggling to find tutorials I am assuming most people are- GIMP is an open source photo editing software similar to Adobe�s Photoshop. The community surrounding these types of software are usually what enrich the learning experience. Learning at all levels is an important no matter how long you have been doing something. I have been so excited to be learning to learn new editing styles and techniques.

Atlanta specifically, is a place that holds dear to my heart especially when it comes to education. I have learned so much in my time here, and I can not wait to see where it takes me. The city as a whole is growing and learning and I feel blessed to have the opportunity to learn and grow from within the community I am in. If you don�t already I would suggest spending more time downtown. During the semester I was riding Marta 5 days a week and walking more than I care to imagine. This also means I got some pretty sick photo walks in during this time. I also took the extra steps to rent some really useful equipment from the GSU CATLab. This was always a commitment that required lugging an honest 30 extra pounds worth of oh-so-bulky photo equipment on and off the train. Only one thing comes to mind though- worth it. There is so much I have to worth with now and so many exciting things in the near future.
